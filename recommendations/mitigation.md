# Mitigation Recommendations

## Phishing Email Response

The following recommendations are based on the findings from this phishing email investigation.



## User Recommendations

- Do not reply to suspicious emails requesting personal information.
- Do not provide government identification documents through email.
- Verify unexpected financial claims through official communication channels.
- Avoid clicking unknown links or opening suspicious attachments.
- Report suspected phishing emails to the appropriate security team or email provider.



## Security Awareness Recommendations

Organizations should educate users about:

- Financial reward scams
- Authority impersonation techniques
- Urgency-based social engineering
- Requests for sensitive information
- Email sender verification



## Technical Recommendations

Organizations should consider implementing:

- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Email filtering solutions
- Anti-phishing protection
- User awareness training
- Email authentication controls such as SPF, DKIM, and DMARC monitoring



## Key Security Lesson

Email authentication can confirm where an email came from, but it cannot determine whether the message itself is trustworthy. Effective phishing detection requires both technical analysis and human awareness.
